Common Phishing Patterns Using Bidi Controls

Common Phishing Patterns Using Bidi Controls

Published: December 20, 2025

Bidirectional spoofing is no longer theoretical. Security researchers and threat intelligence teams track thousands of active campaigns every month that rely on invisible Unicode controls to trick victims.

Here are the most common patterns seen in the wild during 2024 and 2025.

Top 5 Real Attack Types

First, the classic domain reversal: paypal.com reversed to appear as paypal.com. Variations target Apple, Microsoft, Binance, and banks worldwide.

Second, executable hiding: attackers name files Update.pdf.exe but insert LRM or RLM to push the .exe part out of view or make it appear as part of the trusted name.

Third, trusted brand insertion: a comment says “Verified Apple Support” but uses RLI to insert apple.com inside a malicious link, making it look official.

Fourth, nested deception: combining RLO with LRI to reverse only part of a URL while keeping the brand name intact — extremely hard to spot without tools.

Fifth, social media bio attacks: influencers and companies have had their verified account names altered using ALM or RLM to include fake bank details.

These aren’t rare edge cases. Major security vendors including Google, Microsoft, and Cloudflare have published reports confirming widespread use across phishing kits, malicious documents, and compromised websites.
